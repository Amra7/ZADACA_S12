        	<h3>
    		<a href="http://www.archdaily.com/596780/video-zao-standardarchitecture-s-zhange-ke-on-contemplating-basics/" title="Video: ZAO/standardarchitecture&#8217;s Zhang Ke on &#8220;Contemplating Basics&#8221;">Video: ZAO/standardarchitecture&#8217;s Zhang Ke on &#8220;Contemplating Basics&#8221;</a>
    					    	</h3>
		<div class="mtext">
    		<span class="vvqbox vvqvimeo" style="width:530px;height:298px;"><iframe id="vvq-596780-vimeo-1" src="http://player.vimeo.com/video/118393513?title=1&amp;byline=1&amp;portrait=0&amp;fullscreen=1" width="530" height="298" frameborder="0"><a href="http://www.vimeo.com/118393513">http://www.vimeo.com/118393513</a></iframe></span>
<p><!--roulette--></p>
<p class="p1">In conjunction with &#8220;<a href="http://www.archdaily.com/582055/aedes-architecture-forum-highlights-zao-standardarchitecture/">Contemplating Basics</a>,&#8221; the 35th Aedes Architecture Forum&#8217;s <a href="http://www.archdaily.com/tag/exhibition/">exhibition</a> of work by <a href="http://www.archdaily.com/tag/beijing/">Beijing</a>-based <a href="http://www.archdaily.com/tag/zao-standardarchitecture/" target="_blank">ZAO/standardarchitecture</a>, <a href="http://vimeo.com/reframevideos">Reframe</a> presents an interview the firm&#8217;s founder, Zhang Ke, and Dr Eduard Kögel, an Urban Planner and critic from <a href="http://www.archdaily.com/tag/berlin/">Berlin</a>.</p>
<p class="p1">Since its establishment in 2001, ZAO/standardarchitecture has produced a diverse portfolio of projects responding to the specific nature and local culture of their sites, and mediating between traditional values and contemporary means of production. Keenly engaged with social issues, Ke recognises the importance of designing in a manner that is cognisant of broader context and bridges the gap between tradition and modernity.</p>
<p class="p1">&#8220;Every generation of course needs to go back to the original questions&#8230; &#8216;Okay, what architecture can grow out of this place in our time, and with our interaction with the local people and local techniques?&#8217;&#8221; he asks, &#8220;The results could be striking but the departure point is basic.&#8221;</p>
<p class="p1">ZAO/standardarchitecture has been responsible for large urban <a href="http://www.archdaily.com/tag/museum/">museums</a> and small scale rural interventions alike, adopting in all cases this democratic approach to design.</p>
<p class="p1">&#8220;I learnt neither to look up nor to look down,&#8221; Ke said, &#8220;But to look straight in the eye, which means that you truly respect the culture.&#8221;</p>

<p><!--roulette--></p>
<p>The word “<a href="http://www.archdaily.com/tag/treehouse/" class="st_tag internal_tag" rel="tag" title="Posts tagged with treehouse">treehouse</a>” can conjure up fond childhood memories for many.     					    	</h3>
		<div class="mtext">
    		<figure id="newsroom-picture-att-id-54d5360ee58ece14f7000048" aria-labelledby="figcaption_newsroom-picture-att-id-54d5360ee58ece14f7000048" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="/?attachment_id=596082" rel="attachment" class="nr-image nr-picture" data-nr-picture-id="54d5360ee58ece14f7000048" data-nr-image-fp="5d26a8493659f7faffe1d51ac8f9d61f"><img title="Courtesy of THOTH Publishers" class="nr-image nr-picture wp-image-596082" data-nr-picture-id="54d5360ee58ece14f7000048" data-nr-image-fp="5d26a8493659f7faffe1d51ac8f9d61f" src="http://ad009cdnb.archdaily.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/54d5360ee58ece14f7000048_ciam-4-and-the-unanimous-origins-of-modernist-urban-planning_cover_ciam_atlas_lores-530x657.jpg" alt="" width="530" height="657"/></a> <figcaption id="figcaption_newsroom-picture-att-id-54d5360ee58ece14f7000048" class="wp-caption-text"> Courtesy of THOTH Publishers </figcaption></figure>

<p><em>Held in 1933 on a ship in the Mediterranian, the fourth CIAM congress and <a href="http://www.archdaily.com/tag/le-corbusier/" class="st_tag internal_tag" rel="tag" title="Posts tagged with Le Corbusier">Le Corbusier</a>&#8217;s subsequent </em>Athens Charter (La Charte d&#8217;Athenes)<em> are widely regarded as a defining period in Modernist urban planning, a moment when architects came to an agreement on what the future of our cities should look like. But how correct is this interpretation? Edited by Evelien van Es, Gregor Harbusch, Bruno Maurer, Muriel Perez, Kees Somer and Daniel Weiss, a significant new 480-page book, &#8220;<a href="http://bit.ly/ciambook" target="_blank">Atlas of the Functional City - CIAM 4 and Comparative Urban Analysis</a>&#8221; examines the congress in depth. In the following excerpt from the book&#8217;s introduction, Daniel Weiss, Gregor Harbusch and Bruno Maurer examine the commonly accepted history of the congress, finding that support for the underlying principles of <a href="http://www.archdaily.com/tag/modernism/" class="st_tag internal_tag" rel="tag" title="Posts tagged with Modernism">Modernism</a> was perhaps not so unanimous after all.</em></p>
